Unified Thinker serves as a modular reasoning core that bridges the gap between high-level logic and pixel-level synthesis across various generative tasks.

Abstract

Despite impressive progress in high-fidelity image synthesis, generative models still struggle with logic-intensive instruction following, exposing a persistent reasoning-execution gap. We propose Unified Thinker, a task-agnostic reasoning architecture for general image generation, designed as a unified planning core that can plug into diverse generators and workflows. Unified Thinker decouples a dedicated Thinker from the image Generator, enabling modular reasoning upgrades without retraining the entire system. We introduce a two-stage training paradigm: first, constructing a structured planning interface through supervised fine-tuning; second, applying reinforcement learning (RL) to anchor the policy in pixel-level feedback. Our framework demonstrates superior performance across logic-heavy benchmarks, effectively bridging the reasoning-execution gap.

Methodology

  • Decoupled Architecture: A plug-and-play reasoning core (Thinker) that generates structured plans for downstream Generators (e.g., Stable Diffusion, Flux).
  • HieraReason-40K Dataset: A large-scale dataset featuring hierarchical reasoning traces (Intent, Constraints, Sub-goals) for 40,000 image generation and editing tasks.
  • Dual-Phase Training: Combines Joint Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT) with Executable Reinforcement Learning (using H-GRPO) to optimize reasoning based on final image quality.
